Research

My research lies at the intersection of virtual reality, human perception, and behaviour. I focus on scientific research and assessment of human sensory-motor action and perception.

Hardware

One of my strengths is in rapid prototyping cutting edge devices for research and deployment, such as the ATTS touch testing system amongst other.

Software

Most of my hardware projects run in Unity and Unreal game engines, including VR and mobile platforms.

About

As a research scientist and engineer, I believe we are in the midst of a fundamental shift in how we interact with technology. This transformation is largely driven by recent innovations in AI, which has rapidly become our primary interface with digital systems.

This unifying development allows us all to more effectively benefit from the wonders of technology.

My work exists at the intersection of scientific research in human-technology interaction (HTI), encompassing human-computer interaction (HCI), human-robot interaction (HRI), and immersive technologies (e.g., extended reality, XR).

My contributions include:

1. Scientific research and assessment of human sensory-motor action and perception.

2. Development of research-grade hardware for real-time hand and finger tracking, including health and fitness devices that support clinical assessment of human touch and the development of brain-computer interfaces (BCI), incorporating electroencephalography (EEG), electromyography (EMG), electrical muscle stimulation (EMS), pupillometry, and eye tracking.

3. Development of various software research tools, such as phone applications (see Tactile on iOS App Store), XR applications (see SideQuest App Store), and various communication tools for real-time data streaming between robotic devices and game engines like Unity or Unreal Engine.
